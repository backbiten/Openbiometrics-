# Openbiometrics-
For open source development of field biometrics

## Research ethics / acceptable use

This project is intended **exclusively** for opt-in research and usability studies in which participants provide explicit, informed consent before any biometric data is collected or processed.

**This project must not be used for:**
- Covert capture or monitoring in public or private spaces without participants' knowledge and consent
- Individual profiling, tracking, or identification without informed consent
- Mass surveillance or public-space biometric scanning
- Weaponization, harassment, intimidation, or any form of psychological manipulation
- Any application targeting vulnerable populations without appropriate safeguards and oversight

**Users and contributors are expected to:**
- Obtain informed consent from all study participants before collecting any biometric data
- Comply with applicable privacy laws (e.g., GDPR, CCPA, BIPA, HIPAA where relevant)
- Follow institutional review board (IRB) or ethics committee requirements when conducting human-subjects research
- Apply data minimization principles — collect only what is necessary for the stated research purpose
- Securely store, handle, and dispose of participant data in line with [docs/data-policy.md](docs/data-policy.md)

For detailed guidance, see:
- [docs/ethics.md](docs/ethics.md) — Consent requirements, prohibited uses, and data retention guidance
- [docs/data-policy.md](docs/data-policy.md) — Data collection and handling rules
- [docs/threat-model.md](docs/threat-model.md) — Misuse risks and mitigations
